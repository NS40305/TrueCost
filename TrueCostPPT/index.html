TRUECOST

Is This Worth My Time?

THE WHY

(External Motivators: Knowledge Gaps, User Needs, Benefits Sought, Problems)

Knowledge Gap

Prices are presented as abstract dollar amounts.

They do not reflect the time and labor required to earn that money.

Research shows:

  • Users underestimate small, frequent expenses.
  • Users rarely translate price into working hours.
  • Emotional awareness of cost is weak when represented only numerically.

Before Thinking About Time Cost

80% of participants rarely or never consider time cost before purchasing.

User Needs & Behavioral Insights

Users need:

  • Decision support at the moment of purchase
  • A relatable cost metric (time instead of money)
  • Guidance without guilt or pressure

Behavioral Insight:

  • Users respond more strongly to relatable measures (time, effort) than abstract numbers.
  • Decision support is most valuable at the moment of choice.

Benefits Sought & Core Problem

Benefits Sought

  • Reduce impulse purchases
  • Increase awareness of accumulated spending
  • Enable conscious, value-based decisions

Core Problem Statement

"Young adults and students struggle to make informed spending decisions because prices do not reflect the time and effort required to earn the money."

NARROWED
PRIMARY USE CASE

Instead of building a full budgeting system, I narrowed the focus to one core behavioral moment.

Primary Use Case & Simplified Flow

An hourly wage earner is about to purchase a non-essential item and wants to quickly evaluate if it is worth the working time required.

This flow targets the moment of decision, not post-purchase reflection.

SUCCESS
METRICS

To ensure measurable evaluation, I defined three categories:

Success Criteria

1

Usability Success

Users must complete wage-to-time conversion quickly & independently.

Task Completion >= 90% Time On Task <= 30s No Guidance Needed
2

Comprehension Success

Users must clearly understand that "4 hours" equals real labor required.

Correct Explanation >= 90% Meaningfulness >= 4/5
3

Behavioral Influence

At least half of users indicate the conversion changed their thinking.

Reported Reflection >= 50% Decision Pause Observed Intentional Spending >= 50%

PROTOTYPE SUCCESS DEFINITION

The prototype is successful if:

  • Users understand time conversion instantly.
  • Users perceive time cost as more meaningful than dollar price.
  • Users demonstrate hesitation or reflection before purchasing.

"Success is not defined by stopping purchases — It is defined by increasing conscious decision-making."

PROPOSAL STATEMENT

"

I am designing a mobile application that will allow hourly wage earners and students to convert product prices into working hours at the moment of purchase. By visualizing cost in time rather than money, users will increase reflection and make more intentional spending decisions before completing a purchase.
